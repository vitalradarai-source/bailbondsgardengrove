What Happens After an Arrest in Garden Grove

When a Garden Grove Police Department officer makes an arrest, the defendant is transported to the Garden Grove Police Department Jail at 11301 Acacia Parkway, Garden Grove, CA 92840 (phone: 714-741-5704). This city-operated holding facility handles all initial bookings for arrests made within Garden Grove city limits.

Because Garden Grove PD is a smaller city jail, the booking process tends to move faster than at larger county facilities. Many misdemeanor defendants are fully processed and bail-eligible within 1 to 3 hours of arrival. Here is what happens during that window:

Arrival
Transport to Garden Grove PD Jail
The arresting officer completes the arrest report documenting charges, circumstances, and the defendant’s identifying information. The defendant’s phone is taken and secured — this is usually when contact with family cuts off.
Booking
Fingerprints, Photo, Medical Screen
Fingerprints are taken and run through state and federal databases. A booking photo is recorded. California law requires a health screening for every person booked into custody — this checks for injuries, medications, and mental health needs. Any issue found here can add time.
Bail Set
Orange County Bail Schedule Amount Assigned
For most charges, bail is assigned from the Orange County bail schedule published annually by the Superior Court of California, County of Orange. This happens at booking without needing a judge. You can post bail at the schedule amount immediately — before arraignment.
Release or Transfer
Misdemeanor Release or Felony Transfer
Misdemeanor defendants can often post bail directly at Garden Grove PD. Felony defendants are typically transferred to the Orange County Intake Release Center (IRC) at 550 N Flower St, Santa Ana, CA 92703 — or to the Theo Lacy Facility at 501 The City Drive South, Orange, CA 92868. Transfers add 4 to 10 hours before the bond can be posted at the receiving facility.

To search for someone currently in Orange County custody, the OCSD maintains an inmate search at ocsheriff.gov. Records typically appear 1 to 3 hours after booking at Garden Grove PD, and 2 to 4 hours after transfer to a county facility. If you can’t find them yet, call us — we can often confirm location before public databases update.

How a Bail Bond Works — The 10% Fee Explained

When bail is set — whether at $10,000 or $100,000 — most families cannot post the full cash amount to the court. A bail bond is how defendants get released without their family paying the entire bail amount upfront.

Under California Insurance Code § 1800.4, a licensed bail bond agent charges a premium of 10% of the total bail amount. That 10% is a fee paid to the bondsman — it is not a loan, not a deposit, and not money that comes back at the end of the case. In exchange, the bondsman posts a surety bond with the court for the full bail amount, guaranteeing the defendant will appear at every required court date.

Here is what that looks like with real numbers:

Bail Amount Set Bond Premium (10%) What You Pay
$10,000 10% $1,000
$25,000 10% $2,500
$50,000 10% $5,000
$100,000 10% $10,000

The rate is the same whether you call at noon or at midnight. California law does not permit licensed bondsmen to charge after-hours surcharges — the 10% premium is set by statute. If a bondsman quotes you more than 10%, that is a red flag.

Common Charges Behind After-Hours Bail Bond Calls in Garden Grove

Arrests don’t happen on a schedule. Based on our experience serving Orange County, these are the charges that generate the most 24-hour bail bond calls in Garden Grove:

DUI (Vehicle Code § 23152)

DUI arrests spike on weekend nights, holidays, and after events at local venues. A standard first-offense DUI misdemeanor typically carries bail in the $5,000 to $10,000 range under the Orange County bail schedule. DUI causing injury under Vehicle Code § 23153 is a felony and can reach $100,000 or higher. Booking at Garden Grove PD for DUI is often completed within a few hours, making same-night release a realistic goal when a licensed bondsman is on the case immediately.

Domestic Violence (PC § 273.5 and PC § 243(e)(1))

Domestic violence arrests are one of the most common calls we receive after hours. Under California Penal Code § 273.5 (corporal injury to a spouse or cohabitant), felony bail typically ranges from $25,000 to $50,000. Misdemeanor domestic battery under PC § 243(e)(1) usually carries bail around $10,000. There is no statutory 72-hour no-bail hold for domestic violence in California — bail can be posted as soon as booking is complete. A judge may issue a criminal protective order under PC § 136.2 at arraignment, but that is separate from the bail question.

Drug Offenses (Health & Safety Code §§ 11350, 11351)

Simple possession under HS § 11350 is typically a misdemeanor in California after Prop 47 and carries bail in the $2,500 to $5,000 range. Possession for sale under HS § 11351 is a felony with bail commonly set at $25,000. Drug-related arrests in Garden Grove are common given the city’s proximity to major transit corridors in Orange County.

Theft Offenses (PC § 459, PC § 487)

Shoplifting and petty theft are usually misdemeanors with low bail amounts. Grand theft (PC § 487) and residential burglary (PC § 459 first degree) are felonies — first-degree burglary carries $50,000 bail on the Orange County schedule. Commercial burglary (second degree) is typically $20,000.

Assault (PC § 245)

Assault with a deadly weapon under PC § 245(a)(1) carries $30,000 bail on the Orange County schedule. Simple assault and battery misdemeanors are significantly lower. Assault arrests spike at night and on weekends — exactly when you need a bondsman who genuinely answers the phone.

Why 24-Hour Availability Is Not Just a Marketing Line

Many families assume they should wait until morning to figure out bail. That assumption carries real risk. Here is why acting immediately matters:

Arraignment is the deadline

Under California Penal Code § 825, defendants must be brought before a magistrate within 48 hours of arrest (excluding Sundays and court holidays). At arraignment, the judge can — and often does — change the bail amount. For defendants with prior records, serious charges, or flight risk factors, bail can be significantly raised. If you post bail at the schedule amount before arraignment, you lock in that lower number. If you wait and the judge raises bail at arraignment, the cost of getting your loved one out just went up.

Time in custody has real consequences

Every hour a person spends in custody after booking is unnecessary if bail has been assigned and a bond can be posted. Missing work, losing a job, being away from children, the psychological toll of overnight detention — these are costs that mount quickly. A 24-hour bail bond agent services Garden Grove, CA families so that none of those hours are wasted waiting for a business to open.

The transfer window

If a felony defendant is transferred from Garden Grove PD to the Intake Release Center at 4am, a bondsman who only works business hours cannot post at the IRC until 9am at the earliest. That’s five additional hours in county custody for no reason other than the bondsman’s schedule. At Angels Bail Bonds, we track transfers in real time and post at the receiving facility as soon as the defendant arrives — regardless of the hour.
